## Timeline Logic Verification

### The 48-Hour Window Mechanics
**Established Parameters**:
- Death confirmed: June 15, 11:52 AM GMT
- Resurrection procedure: 48-hour minimum for neural reconstruction
- Smart will execution: Immediate upon death confirmation
- Asset transfer completion: Within 6 hours of death

**Consistency Requirements**:
- All blockchain networks must recognize death simultaneously
- Asset transfers must complete before resurrection attempt
- Legal status change must be irreversible once transfers complete
- Revival procedure must be technically constrained to minimum 48 hours

**Potential Contradictions to Monitor**:
- Could emergency resurrection be attempted earlier?
- What if death determination was disputed in real-time?
- How do international legal systems reconcile with blockchain law?
- Could medical emergency override smart contract execution?

### Asset Transfer Cascade Timing
**Technical Constraints**:
- Complex corporate restructuring requires settlement time
- International transfers must clear multiple blockchain networks
- Employee stock allocations need verification protocols
- Patent database updates require consensus confirmation

**Logical Requirements**:
- Kaelen must have pre-positioned all legal structures
- Transfer protocols must be pre-programmed into smart contracts
- No human intervention possible once death triggers system
- All transfers must be legally valid in multiple jurisdictions

**Questions to Resolve**:
- How did Kaelen gain write access to Damon's smart will?
- What legal structure allowed such comprehensive redistribution?
- How were employee stock allocations pre-calculated and verified?
- What prevents traditional courts from challenging blockchain transfers?

## Consistency Resolution Mechanisms

### Decision Framework for Contradictions
When internal contradictions arise:
1. **Prioritize Character Logic**: Human psychology over technical convenience
2. **Maintain System Rules**: Established mechanics cannot be violated
3. **Check Historical Precedent**: Similar situations in real history
4. **Apply Stephenson Test**: Would this complexity excite or confuse readers?
5. **Preserve Moral Ambiguity**: Avoid simple heroes/villains solutions

### Documentation Requirements
For each major plot point:
- **Technical Feasibility**: How could this actually work?
- **Psychological Plausibility**: Would characters really act this way?
- **Historical Parallel**: Has anything similar happened before?
- **Systemic Logic**: Does this fit established world rules?
- **Narrative Function**: Why is this scene/element necessary?

### Consistency Review Checkpoints
Before writing each Act:
- **Character Arc Verification**: Are all character developments earned?
- **Technical System Check**: Do all mechanisms work as established?
- **Timeline Logic Review**: Does the sequence of events make sense?
- **Cause-Effect Validation**: Does each event logically enable the next?
- **Reader Understanding**: Can audiences follow the logical chain?
