## Energy Economics (AS SHOWN IN FRAMING DEVICE)

### The Renewable Integration Cost Problem (Lines 37-51, Framing Device)

**Core Constraint on 2050 Economy:**
- By 2050, developed world achieved net-zero emissions targets
- Method: Outsourced energy-intensive manufacturing to China
- China manufactured renewables (solar, wind, batteries) using coal power
- Accounting worked at point-of-use, not point-of-production

**Non-Linear Integration Costs (Australia Example):**
- South Australia by 2040: 70% renewables
- Experienced "price volatility" (euphemism for chaos):
  - Negative wholesale prices during sunny midday (excess generation)
  - Extreme spikes during evening "ramping period" (solar gone, demand peaks)
- Marginal generation cost → zero on sunny afternoons
- Total system cost remained "stubbornly high"

**Why Costs Stay High:**
1. Must maintain expensive backup generation capacity (sits idle most of time)
2. Must build massive storage infrastructure (uneconomical at scale)
3. Must over-build transmission to move power where needed
4. Integration costs grow exponentially with renewable penetration

**Impact on AI Economics:**
- Token-based LLM query: electricity ~= human knowledge worker for 1 hour
- Training state-of-the-art model: electricity = small city's annual usage
- **At prevailing energy prices**: Human labor more economical than AI replacement
- Hybrid economy: AI augments humans rather than replacing wholesale

**CRITICAL PLOT HOLE**: This energy constraint is established, then never mentioned again. When abundance emerges post-Liberation, does energy scarcity remain? Are we in partial post-scarcity? This is unresolved.
